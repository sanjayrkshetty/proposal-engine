# Discovery Brief — Karnataka Bank
**Service:** DFIR Retainer — Enterprise  
**Recommended Tier:** Enterprise — The Enterprise tier is recommended due to the bank's large size, complex IT infrastructure, and need for dedicated cybersecurity expertise to meet RBI cyber resilience requirements.

## Pain Points
- Insufficient incident response capabilities
- Lack of dedicated cybersecurity expertise
- Inability to meet RBI cyber resilience requirements
- Limited visibility into security threats
- Inadequate security incident response planning

## Regulatory Drivers
- RBI Cyber Security Framework
- Information Technology Act 2000
- Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard (PCI-DSS)
- ISO 27001

## Environment Assumptions
- Complex IT infrastructure with multiple branches and online banking systems
- Large employee base with varying levels of cybersecurity awareness
- High-volume financial transactions and sensitive customer data
- Existing security controls and incident response processes in place

## Top Discovery Questions
1. What is the current incident response process and how is it currently handled?
2. What are the biggest cybersecurity concerns for the bank's leadership?
3. What is the current security posture and are there any existing security controls?
4. How does the bank currently monitor and detect security threats?
5. What are the key performance indicators (KPIs) for measuring the effectiveness of the DFIR retainer?

## Urgency Signals
- Recent security incidents or breaches
- Upcoming RBI audits or assessments
- Increased cybersecurity threats in the banking industry
- New security requirements or regulations
- Board-level concern for cybersecurity
